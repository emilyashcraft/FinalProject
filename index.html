About Our Mission

This project explores the impact of disability representation in film. It highlights the importance of authentic storytelling in shaping societal perceptions and breaking harmful stereotypes.

Accurate representation fosters empathy and helps normalize diversity in mainstream media, paving the way for a more inclusive society. Here is why this is important:

  • Expands Opportunities for Individuals with Disabilities: Accurate representation encourages the film industry to cast actors with disabilities and create roles that reflect their authentic experiences.
  • Increases Visibility in Media: Ethical portrayals ensure individuals with disabilities are seen as dynamic, multidimensional characters, rather than being erased or misrepresented on screen.
  • Encourages Inclusive Filmmaking: Prioritizing representation inspires filmmakers to collaborate with individuals with disabilities, amplifying their voices and creative contributions.
  • Challenges Industry Norms: Highlighting diverse stories disrupts longstanding biases and stereotypes perpetuated by Hollywood, paving the way for cultural and systemic change.
  • Connects Audiences to Real Stories: Films that portray authentic disability narratives educate audiences, fostering empathy and breaking down societal barriers.

Case Studies

Good Examples of Representation

Crip Camp: A Disability Revolution (2020)

Why: This documentary explores the disability rights movement through the lens of a summer camp for teens with disabilities. It empowers individuals with disabilities to share their own stories, emphasizing activism and community.
Authentic Casting: The documentary features real individuals with disabilities who were part of the disability rights movement.

The Peanut Butter Falcon (2019)

Why: Starring Zack Gottsagen, an actor with Down syndrome, this film authentically depicts his character's journey and abilities, without reducing him to stereotypes or pity.
Authentic Casting: Zack Gottsagen, an actor with Down syndrome, plays the lead role.

Sound of Metal (2019)

Why: This film explores hearing loss and the Deaf community with nuance, particularly through the protagonist’s journey of acceptance and the inclusion of Deaf actors.
Authentic Casting: Many members of the Deaf community, including actor Chelsea Lee, are featured.

CODA (2021)

Why: Featuring a predominantly Deaf cast, this film authentically portrays the dynamics of a Deaf family and their hearing child, celebrating Deaf culture while exploring the challenges of bridging two worlds..
Authentic Casting: Marlee Matlin, Troy Kotsur, and Daniel Durant, who are all Deaf, play pivotal roles.

Temple Grandin (2010)

Why: This biographical film authentically portrays Dr. Temple Grandin, a woman with autism spectrum disorder, and her groundbreaking contributions to animal science. The story focuses on her strengths rather than framing autism as a limitation.
Authentic Casting: Claire Danes does not have autism but plays the role of Dr. Temple Grandin. Although it would be a better example if the casting were authentic, Temple Grandin herself was heavily involved in the film’s production, ensuring the portrayal of her life and autism was accurate.


Poor Examples of Representation


Rain Man (1988)

Why: While Dustin Hoffman’s performance as a man with autism spectrum disorder received acclaim, the film perpetuates harmful stereotypes, such as the "savant genius," and focuses more on the neurotypical character's journey.

Me Before You (2016)

Why: The film presents the protagonist’s physical disability (quadriplegia) as a tragedy so insurmountable that euthanasia is framed as the only solution, perpetuating ableist narratives.

Forrest Gump (1994)

Why: While beloved by many, the film simplifies Forrest's intellectual disability, reducing him to a feel-good caricature and avoiding deeper exploration of his lived experience.

Hunchback of Notre Dame (1996)

Why: Disney’s portrayal of Quasimodo oversimplifies his physical disability and societal exclusion, relying on pity rather than empowerment.
